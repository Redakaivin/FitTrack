BMI Ranges & What They Mean

BMI Range Category Health Risk
< 18.5UnderweightHigher risk (malnutrition, weak immunity)
18.5 - 24.9NormalLow risk (healthy range)
25.0- 29.9OverweightModerate risk (heart issues, diabetes)
30.0 - 34.9Obese Class 1Elevated risk (hypertension, stroke)
35.0 - 39.9Obese Class 2High risk (chronic conditions)
≥ 40.0Obese Class 3Very high risk (severe complications)

*BMI is just one indicator. Muscle mass, diet, and lifestyle matter too.

Quick Health Facts

  • BMI gives a quick estimate of body fat but doesnt reflect muscle vs. fat.
  • Waist size often predicts health risk better than BMI alone.
  • Even small fitness changes can improve health, not just your weight.
  • Athletes can have a “high” BMI because muscle weighs more than fat.
  • The best BMI range for most adults is 18.5 - 24.9, but body type matters.
